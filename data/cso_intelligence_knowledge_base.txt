Title: Draft Amendment - Digital Asset Service Provider Framework
Date: 2026-06-25

A draft amendment to the digital asset service provider framework proposes stricter capital adequacy requirements for custodial wallet providers, raising the minimum capital threshold from $500,000 to $2M for providers holding client assets above $10M, effective Q1 2027. Public consultation closes in 30 days, and early industry feedback flags concern over compliance cost for smaller providers, some of whom may exit the market or seek licensing in less demanding jurisdictions. The draft also introduces mandatory insurance coverage requirements and a new incident reporting obligation within 24 hours for material security events. Legal advisors note that the proposed thresholds are above those in comparable frameworks in Singapore and Dubai, which may create a competitive disadvantage for smaller, innovative custodians. A balanced consultation response that advocates for proportionate thresholds while retaining the intent of enhanced consumer protection is recommended. The outcome of this consultation will significantly shape the competitive landscape for digital asset custody and the long-term attractiveness of our jurisdiction for crypto-native and institutional digital asset businesses.

Title: Fund Reporting Standards - ESG Disclosure Enhancement
Date: 2026-06-05

Revised ESG disclosure standards for fund managers take effect in September 2026, requiring quarterly rather than annual reporting on a defined set of 18 sustainability metrics for funds above $250M AUM, and annual reporting for all registered funds above $50M AUM. The new standards align with ISSB guidance and are broadly consistent with the EU SFDR framework, creating potential mutual recognition opportunities with European regulators for cross-border fund distribution.
